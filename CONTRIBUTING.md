# Contributing

This is a practical playbook for non-technical founders. Every contribution should make the path from idea → first paying customer **shorter and clearer** for someone reading at 11pm with a half-formed product in their head.

## Ways to contribute

### Spotted a typo or broken link?
Open a PR. One-line PRs are welcome.

### Have a real founder story to share?
The most valuable additions to this library are **real-world examples** — founders who shipped fast, mispriced badly, validated the wrong way, or closed their first customer through a weird channel. If you have a story (yours or someone else's, with permission), open an issue or PR with:

- **What stage** the story belongs to (Chapter 1–10)
- **What happened** in 3–5 sentences
- **The lesson** — what other founders should take from it

### Disagree with something?
Good. Open an issue with the chapter, the section, and your counter-argument. Strong opinions, loosely held.

### Want to translate a chapter?
We'd love to see translations. Open an issue first to claim a language so we don't end up with five Spanish versions.

## What we won't merge

- Promotional content for unrelated products or services
- Generic startup platitudes ("just build something people want!") with no substance
- AI-generated filler — every paragraph should earn its place
- Anything that softens the core message: ship fast, charge early, talk to users

## Style rules

If you're contributing prose:

- **Short sentences.** 3–4 sentences max per paragraph.
- **Specific numbers, always.** "$3k–$6k" not "a few thousand". "21 days" not "a few weeks".
- **Outcomes, never features.** A founder reading this cares about what happens to their business, not what tool you used.
- **Cite real sources.** YC lectures, Garry Tan posts, founder interviews — link them. No anonymous "studies show".

## Star the repo

If this library helps you ship — even just one chapter — please star the repo. It's the single best signal that this work is worth continuing.
