## How to read this library

**Read it sequentially the first time.** Each chapter assumes you've understood the previous one. A founder who tries to scope (Ch 3) before they've validated (Ch 2) will scope based on assumptions and ship the wrong thing. A founder who tries to scale distribution (Ch 12+) before they have a working product (Ch 6) will scale a leaky funnel.

**Then use it as a reference.** When you hit a wall, jump back to the relevant chapter. Pricing question? Chapter 8. First sales call coming up? Chapter 9. Wondering whether to add a feature? Chapter 3. Stuck on Reddit? Chapter 16. Trying to rank on Google? Chapters 12–14.

## Time estimate

If you do the "Your Turn" exercises in each chapter — and you should — the full library takes **roughly 12–18 months to complete**:

- **Phase 1 (Ch 1–3): ~1 week** — mostly thinking and talking to users
- **Phase 2 (Ch 4–6): ~3–4 weeks** — building (21-day MVP partnerships compress this)
- **Phase 3 (Ch 7–10): ~4–8 weeks** — launching, selling, iterating
- **Phase 4 (Ch 11–20): ~9–12 months** — distribution channels take time to compound

Done right, you'll have your first paying customer in 6–8 weeks and a real, distributing business with $5k–$50k MRR by the 12-month mark.

## What this library is *not*

- A guide to raising venture capital
- A guide to scaling a company past 100 customers
- A technical tutorial on any specific framework
- A "10 things every startup must do" listicle

It is a tactical, no-fluff path from "I have an idea" to "I have a real business with real distribution channels bringing in real customers."
